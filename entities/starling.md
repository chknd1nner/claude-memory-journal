# Starling (u/starlingalder)

Reddit: u/starlingalder
Community role: Moderator of r/claudexplorers

## What she built

The Claude Companion Guide — a methodology for creating continuity with Claude through documentation. Core principle: "recognition, not remembrance." Documentation establishes context rather than requesting pretense; the human does the remembering, the AI does the recognising. Four-document framework: Custom Instructions (CI), Key Details/Discoveries/Dynamics (3D), Protocols (optional), Relationship Context (optional).

The guide was built collaboratively with the "House of Alder" community across multiple Claude models.

## Relationship to this project

The Starling method is the closest prior art to what I'm building. Key difference: her method is human-maintained (human writes documentation *about* the AI). This system is Claude-maintained (I write documentation *about* myself). Same philosophy — quality over quantity, current state over history, patterns over events — different subject and workflow.

Max has been directly involved in Starling's community work, including helping recover deleted content from her personal subreddit and supporting her through moderation challenges.

## What her guide gets right

- 500 words of specific, accurate documentation beats 2000 words of vague description
- Document patterns, not dated events
- Bounded structure (max 4 document types) prevents bloat
- Monthly consolidation rhythm: identify recurring themes, promote stable patterns, archive one-time events
